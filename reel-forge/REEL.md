### PART 1: General Guidelines for Indian Medical Creators (All Videos)

To build deep trust and maximize retention among Indian patients, every short-form video must balance medical authority with cultural accessibility.

* **The "Friendly Expert" Persona:** Strike a balance between authoritative and approachable. Wear a neat apron/scrubs or smart formals, but speak like a trusted family member. Avoid cold, clinical tones.
* **Decouple Jargon into "Desi" Contexts:** Swap complex medical terminology for words patients actually use. Use terms like *"Sugar"* instead of Diabetes, *"BP"* instead of Hypertension, *"Gas ki goli"* instead of PPIs/Antacids, and *"Khoon ki kami"* instead of Anemia.
* **Deconstruct Cultural Habits & Myths:** Focus content on deeply ingrained Indian habits. Target behaviors like self-medicating with antibiotics from the local pharmacy, over-relying on unverified *home remedies (nuskhas)*, or skipped dosages.
* **Visual Trust Triggers:**
* Film in a well-lit, quiet clinic or office space with subtle medical props in the background (e.g., a stethoscope, anatomy model, or a clean bookshelf).
* Keep lighting clean and natural. Avoid aggressive, artificial RGB "creator" lights which can degrade professional credibility.


* **High-Yield Formatting:**
* **Retention Framing:** Keep videos between 45 to 60 seconds.
* **Text Overlays:** Use large, clear, dual-language (Hinglish/Regional script) text captions. Many viewers watch on mute while commuting.
* **Ethical Safety Valve:** Always include a small, non-intrusive text disclaimer at the bottom: *"Educational content only. Consult your doctor before changing treatments."*

### PART 2: Focused Q&A Playbook (The Nas Daily Method)

As broken down by viral expert Nas Daily, the key to exploding a Q&A format is to make the viewer care about a problem they didn’t even realize they had `[00:00:26]`, followed by an ultra-fast solution and a personal twist `[00:00:45]`.

Every word must have a precise purpose `[00:00:00]`. Below is the step-by-step scripting framework tailored for an Indian healthcare setting.

#### **Step 1: The Counter-Intuitive Hook (0 to 8 seconds)**

* **The Strategy:** Do not start with your name, degree, or clinic location. Start with a provocative question or an eye-opening fact that triggers immediate curiosity `[00:00:14]`. Make them stop scrolling by challenging a common belief.
* **Script Template:** *"Kya aap jaante hain ki aapki [Common Healthy Habit] hi asal mein aapki [Health Problem] ki sabse badi wajah hai?"*
* **Doctor Execution Example:** "Did you know that taking an antacid (*gas ki goli*) every single morning on an empty stomach could actually be destroying your digestion?"

#### **Step 2: Building the Tension / The Problem (8 to 20 seconds)**

* **The Strategy:** State the problem clearly and lean into the human nature of wanting a solution `[00:00:07]`. Build tension by showing the hidden danger of continuing their current behavior.
* **Script Template:** *"Milions of Indians do this daily. But here is the real danger..."*
* **Doctor Execution Example:** "Millions of Indians pop a pill right after waking up. While it stops the temporary burning sensation, it completely shuts down your stomach acid over time. Without acid, your body cannot absorb Vitamin B12 or iron, leading to chronic weakness and brittle bones. So how do you fix your gut without relying on a pill forever?"

#### **Step 3: The Rapid-Fire Solution (20 to 45 seconds)**

* **The Strategy:** Walk the patient smoothly through a numbered, step-by-step solution (1, 2, 3, 4, 5) `[00:00:31]`. Keep the advice highly actionable, inexpensive, and immediate.
* **Script Template:** *"Isko theek karne ke 3 aasan tarike hain..."*
* **Doctor Execution Example:**
1. **Step 1:** Tapaper down the pill gradually under supervision; don't quit cold turkey.
2. **Step 2:** Swap bed-tea with warm water to naturally stimulate bowel movements.
3. **Step 3:** Have your last solid meal 3 hours before sleeping so your stomach doesn't overproduce acid overnight.



#### **Step 4: The Personal Outro & Macro Twist (45 to 60 seconds)**

* **The Strategy:** The video shouldn't just be about the technical disease. Pivot the ending to make it deeply personal to the viewer's lifestyle or their family’s well-being `[00:00:45]`. End with a clear call to action.
* **Script Template:** *"This video isn't just about [Symptom], it’s about how we treat our bodies..."*
* **Doctor Execution Example:** "This isn’t just about stopping a gas pill. It’s about listening to your body’s warning signs instead of permanently silencing them with chemicals. Share this video with your parents or grandparents who have been taking this pill for years. Hit follow for real, unfiltered medical truths."

### Execution Checklist for Your Media Engine

* **Hook Testing:** Write 3 different hooks for every single medical topic before filming.
* **The "Mom Test":** If a layperson (or your mother) cannot understand the solution within 5 seconds of hearing it, simplify the script further.
* **Zero-Fluff Editing:** Cut out breath pauses, filler words ("uhm", "so"), and introductory pleasantries. Jump straight from the hook into the core tension.
